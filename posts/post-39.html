📊 The Design Challenge

Chemical process design is a complex task that involves balancing multiple objectives including efficiency, cost, safety, and environmental impact. Traditional design approaches often rely on experience and trial-and-error methods that may not achieve optimal results. Many processes are designed with suboptimal configurations that waste energy and resources. Modern optimization techniques offer new approaches to overcome these challenges.

📊 The Efficiency Problem

Traditional process design methods may not achieve optimal efficiency, leading to processes that consume more energy and resources than necessary. This increases operating costs and environmental impact.

⏱️ The Cost Challenge

Many chemical processes are designed without comprehensive cost optimization, leading to higher capital and operating costs than necessary. This can make processes economically unviable or reduce profitability.
