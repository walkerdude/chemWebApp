🔬 The Reaction Optimization Challenge

Chemical reactions are complex processes that depend on many factors including temperature, pressure, concentration, and catalyst properties. Traditional approaches to reaction optimization often rely on trial-and-error methods or simplified models that may not capture the full complexity of real systems.

📊 The Modeling Problem

Traditional reaction models often use simplified assumptions that may not accurately represent real reaction systems. This can lead to suboptimal process conditions and reduced efficiency.

⏱️ The Control Challenge

Many chemical reactions are sensitive to process conditions and may require precise control to achieve desired outcomes. Traditional control methods may not provide the level of precision needed for optimal performance.
